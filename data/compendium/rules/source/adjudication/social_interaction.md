# Social Interaction

Social interaction happens when adventurers deal with NPCs or creatures that are willing to talk instead of fight. An NPC's attitude toward the characters is Friendly, Indifferent, or Hostile.

Social interaction proceeds through:

- roleplaying
- ability checks when the GM wants chance to affect the outcome

Roleplaying matters. The GM uses the NPC's personality and the characters' words and actions to determine how the NPC reacts. Offers, flattery, fear, sympathy, insults, and shared goals can all shift an interaction.

When dice are needed, the GM will usually call for the Influence action. Players should choose approaches that fit the proficiencies available in the party.

Friendly NPCs are predisposed to help. Hostile NPCs are predisposed to hinder. Indifferent NPCs are neither strongly helpful nor strongly obstructive by default.
