# Saving Throws

A saving throw represents resisting or evading a threat such as poison, force, fire, or magic. A creature usually makes a saving throw because an effect forces it to do so, not because the creature chooses to.

A creature can choose to fail a saving throw without rolling if it does not want to resist the effect.

Saving throws are named for the ability modifier they use:

- Strength: physically resist direct force
- Dexterity: dodge out of harm's way
- Constitution: endure a toxic or bodily hazard
- Intelligence: recognize an illusion as fake
- Wisdom: resist a mental assault
- Charisma: assert identity or presence of self

Add Proficiency Bonus to a saving throw only if the creature is proficient in that kind of save.

The Difficulty Class for a saving throw is determined by the effect that causes it or by the GM. Spell saves use the caster's spell save DC, and monster abilities that require saves specify their own DC.
