# Rhythm of Play

The three main pillars of play are social interaction, exploration, and combat. Regardless of which pillar is active, play follows a repeating three-step loop:

1. The Game Master describes the scene.
   The GM explains where the adventurers are and what is around them.
2. The players describe what their characters do.
   Outside combat, the GM decides how to resolve overlapping activity and ensures each character gets a chance to act. In combat, creatures take turns.
3. The GM narrates the results.
   If an attempted task is straightforward, the GM can simply describe the result. If a task is uncertain and meaningful, the GM can call for a die roll to determine what happens.

The result of step 3 usually creates a new decision point, which returns play to step 1.
