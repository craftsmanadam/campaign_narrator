# Making an Attack

When a creature takes the Attack action, it makes an attack. Some other actions, Bonus Actions, Reactions, spells, and features also allow attacks.

Attack sequence:

1. Choose a target within range.
2. Determine modifiers.
   - check for cover
   - determine Advantage or Disadvantage
   - apply penalties or bonuses from spells, features, and other effects
3. Resolve the attack roll.
   - on a hit, roll damage unless the attack says otherwise
   - some attacks impose effects instead of or in addition to damage

Unseen attackers and targets:

- when attacking a target you cannot see, you have Disadvantage
- if the target is not actually in the chosen location, the attack misses
- when a creature cannot see you, you have Advantage on attack rolls against it
- if you were hidden when you attack, your location is revealed whether the attack hits or misses

Cover:

- Half Cover: `+2` bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws
- Three-Quarters Cover: `+5` bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws
- Total Cover: cannot be targeted directly

Only the best available cover applies; cover benefits do not stack together.
