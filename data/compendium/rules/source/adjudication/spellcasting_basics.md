# Spellcasting Basics

This file captures the general spellcasting rules that apply across classes, monsters, and magic items.

## Access to Spells

To cast a spell, a creature must:

- have it prepared,
- know it through a feature,
- or access it through a magic item.

Some spells are always prepared and do not count against prepared-spell limits.

The SRD also defines how prepared-spell lists change by class:

- Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock: change one spell when gaining a level
- Cleric, Druid, Wizard: can change any prepared spells after a Long Rest
- Paladin, Ranger: can replace one spell after a Long Rest

## Spell Levels and Slots

- Cantrips are level 0 and use no spell slot.
- Level 1+ spells usually expend a spell slot.
- A spell can be cast using a higher-level slot, and some spells improve when that happens.
- Finishing a Long Rest restores expended spell slots.
- Spell schools are Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, and Transmutation.

## One Slotted Spell per Turn

On a turn, you can expend only one spell slot to cast a spell. This prevents using both an action spell and a bonus-action spell with slots on the same turn.

## Casting Times

Common casting times in the first cut:

- Action
- Bonus Action
- Reaction
- 1 minute or more
- Ritual

Spells with longer casting times require the caster to keep taking the Magic action on each turn and maintain Concentration until the casting completes. If Concentration is broken, the spell fails and the slot is not expended.

## Range Categories

- Distance in feet
- Touch
- Self

If a spell has movable effects, they are not restricted by the spell's range unless the spell says otherwise.

## Components

- Verbal: normal speaking voice required
- Somatic: at least one free hand for gestures
- Material: specific materials, a component pouch, or a permitted spellcasting focus

For Material components:

- if the spell does not consume them and gives no cost, a Component Pouch can replace them
- a Spellcasting Focus can replace them only if the caster has a feature allowing it
- The spellcaster must have a hand free to access materials or a pouch
- the same hand can be used for both Somatic and Material components

## Duration Types

- Instantaneous
- Time span
- Concentration

While a time-span spell you cast is ongoing, you can dismiss it with no action required if you do not have the Incapacitated condition.

## Targeting Rules

- A spell needs a clear path to its target unless the spell says otherwise.
- A caster cannot target through Total Cover.
- Areas of effect use shapes such as Cone, Cube, Cylinder, Emanation, Line, and Sphere.
- A creature does not automatically know it was targeted unless the spell has a perceptible effect.
- Invalid targets still consume the spell slot when the spell is cast.

## Spell Save DC and Spell Attack Modifier

- Spell save DC: `8 + spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus`
- Spell attack modifier: `spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus`

## Combined Spell Effects

Different spells stack. Multiple copies of the same spell do not stack; only the most potent active copy applies.

## Identifying Ongoing Spells

Use the Study action and succeed on a `DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana)` check to identify a visible non-instantaneous spell by its effects.
