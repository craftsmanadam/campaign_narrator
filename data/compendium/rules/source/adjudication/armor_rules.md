# Armor Rules

Armor is grouped into `Light`, `Medium`, `Heavy`, and `Shield`. Armor entries define base Armor Class, Strength thresholds when relevant, Stealth penalties when relevant, don and doff times, weight, and cost.

## Armor Training

Anyone can don armor or hold a Shield, but training is required to use them effectively.

### Light, Medium, or Heavy Armor

If you wear Light, Medium, or Heavy armor and lack training with it:

- you have Disadvantage on any D20 Test that involves Strength or Dexterity
- you can't cast spells

### Shield

You gain the Armor Class benefit of a Shield only if you have training with it.

## One at a Time

A creature can wear only one suit of armor at a time and wield only one Shield at a time.

## Armor Class and Category Rules

- Light armor uses a base value plus full Dexterity modifier
- Medium armor uses a base value plus Dexterity modifier up to a maximum of `2`
- Heavy armor uses a fixed Armor Class
- Shield adds `+2 AC`

## Strength and Stealth

Some Heavy armor reduces Speed by `10 feet` unless the wearer meets the listed Strength score. Some armor imposes Disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks.

## Don and Doff Times

- Light armor: `1 minute` to don or doff
- Medium armor: `5 minutes` to don, `1 minute` to doff
- Heavy armor: `10 minutes` to don, `5 minutes` to doff
- Shield: `Utilize` action to don or doff
