# Monk

## Core Traits

- Primary ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
- Hit Point Die: `d8`
- Saves: Strength, Dexterity
- Skills: choose `2` from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, or Stealth
- Armor: none
- Weapons: Simple weapons and Martial weapons with the Light property
- Tool: one Artisan's Tool or Musical Instrument
- Starting equipment: Spear, `5 Daggers`, tool choice, Explorer's Pack, and `11 GP`; or `50 GP`

## Level 1 to 5

- Level 1: `Martial Arts`, `Unarmored Defense`
- Level 2: `Monk's Focus`, `Unarmored Movement`, `Uncanny Metabolism`
- Level 3: `Deflect Attacks`, Monk subclass
- Level 4: feat or Ability Score Improvement, `Slow Fall`
- Level 5: `Extra Attack`, `Stunning Strike`

## Core Combat Identity

- Bonus Action Unarmed Strike while meeting Monk weapon and armor restrictions
- Martial Arts die starts at `1d6`
- Can use Dexterity for unarmed and Monk-weapon attacks
- Unarmored Defense sets base AC to `10 + Dexterity modifier + Wisdom modifier`

## Focus Points

Your focus and martial training allow you to harness a well of extraordinary energy within yourself.

- At level 2, Focus Points: `2`
- At level 3, Focus Points: `3`
- At level 5, Focus Points: `5`
- save DC for Focus features is `8 + Wisdom modifier + Proficiency Bonus`

The Monk's main class resource powers:

- `Flurry of Blows`
- `Patient Defense`
- `Step of the Wind`

This should be tracked explicitly in runtime state.

- `Flurry of Blows`: spend `1 Focus Point` to make two Unarmed Strikes as a Bonus Action
- `Patient Defense`: Bonus Action Disengage, or spend `1 Focus Point` to Disengage and Dodge
- `Step of the Wind`: Bonus Action Dash, or spend `1 Focus Point` to Dash and Disengage and double jump distance
- `Uncanny Metabolism`: when you roll Initiative, regain all expended Focus Points and heal `Monk level + Martial Arts die`, once per Long Rest

## Subclass in the SRD

`Warrior of the Open Hand`

- Level 3: `Open Hand Technique`
- Level 6: `Wholeness of Body`
- Level 11: `Fleet Step`
- Level 17: `Quivering Palm`

## First-Cut Use

For the prototype, the Monk is important because it stresses:

- Bonus Action economy,
- unarmed combat rules,
- and condition-heavy tactical effects like Stunned and Prone.
