# Weapon Properties

The SRD weapon rules distinguish between weapon properties and mastery properties. Properties are inherent traits of the weapon. Mastery properties require a feature that unlocks them.

## Core Weapon Properties

### Ammunition

A weapon with `Ammunition` can make a ranged attack only if the required ammunition is available. Each attack expends one piece of ammunition, and drawing that ammunition is part of the attack. After a fight, spending 1 minute recovers half the ammunition used, rounded down.

### Finesse

When making an attack with a `Finesse` weapon, choose Strength or Dexterity for both the attack roll and the damage roll.

### Heavy

You have Disadvantage on attack rolls with a `Heavy` weapon if:

- it is a Melee weapon and your Strength is less than `13`, or
- it is a Ranged weapon and your Dexterity is less than `13`.

### Light

When you take the Attack action on your turn and attack with a Light weapon, you can make one extra attack as a Bonus Action later on the same turn. That extra attack must use a different Light weapon, and you do not add your ability modifier to the extra attack's damage unless the modifier is negative.

### Loading

You can fire only one piece of ammunition from a `Loading` weapon when you use an action, Bonus Action, or Reaction to fire it, regardless of how many attacks you can normally make.

### Range

A ranged entry lists a normal range and a long range. Attacking beyond normal range gives Disadvantage. Attacking beyond long range is not allowed.

### Reach

A `Reach` weapon adds `5 feet` to your reach for attacks and Opportunity Attacks.

### Thrown

A `Thrown` weapon can be thrown to make a ranged attack, and it can be drawn as part of the attack. A thrown Melee weapon uses the same ability modifier that its melee attack would use.

### Two-Handed

A `Two-Handed` weapon requires two hands when you attack with it.

### Versatile

A `Versatile` weapon can be used with one or two hands. The higher listed damage applies when used with two hands for a melee attack.

## Mastery Properties

### Cleave

If you hit a creature with a melee attack roll using this weapon, you can make one additional melee attack roll with the weapon against a second creature within 5 feet of the first and also within your reach. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon's damage, but you do not add your ability modifier unless it is negative. This extra attack can happen only once per turn.

### Graze

If your attack roll with this weapon misses, you can deal damage equal to the ability modifier used for the attack roll. The damage is the weapon's normal damage type.

### Nick

When you make the extra attack granted by the `Light` property, you can make it as part of the Attack action instead of as a Bonus Action. This can happen only once per turn.

### Push

If you hit a Large or smaller creature with this weapon, you can push it up to `10 feet` straight away from yourself.

### Sap

If you hit a creature with this weapon, that creature has Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn.

### Slow

If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage, you can reduce its Speed by `10 feet` until the start of your next turn. Multiple hits do not stack beyond `10 feet`.

### Topple

If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force it to make a Constitution saving throw with DC `8 + attack ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus`. On a failure, the creature has the Prone condition.

### Vex

If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage, you have Advantage on your next attack roll against that creature before the end of your next turn.
