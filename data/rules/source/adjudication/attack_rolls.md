# Attack Rolls

An attack roll determines whether an attack hits a target. It hits if the total equals or exceeds the target's Armor Class.

Attack roll ability usage:

- Strength: melee attack with a weapon or an Unarmed Strike
- Dexterity: ranged attack with a weapon
- Varies: spell attack, using the spellcasting ability granted by the caster's feature

Some features override these defaults. For example, the Finesse property allows a melee weapon attack to use Strength or Dexterity.

Add Proficiency Bonus to an attack roll when:

- attacking with a weapon you are proficient with
- making a spell attack

Armor Class starts from this base calculation:

`Base AC = 10 + Dexterity modifier`

Armor, spells, class features, and other rules can change AC. If multiple features offer different base AC calculations, choose only one of them.

Natural d20 rules for attack rolls:

- natural `20`: the attack hits regardless of modifiers or AC; this is a critical hit
- natural `1`: the attack misses regardless of modifiers or AC
