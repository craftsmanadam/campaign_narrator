# Equipment Overview

Equipment includes coins, weapons, armor, tools, adventuring gear, mounts, spellcasting services, and magic-item handling rules. For the first-cut prototype, the most important operational rules are coin values, selling value, weapon proficiency, armor training, tool proficiency, and the use of common adventuring gear.

## Coins

The Gold Piece is the main reference unit.

- Copper Piece (`CP`): `1/100 GP`
- Silver Piece (`SP`): `1/10 GP`
- Electrum Piece (`EP`): `1/2 GP`
- Gold Piece (`GP`): `1 GP`
- Platinum Piece (`PP`): `10 GP`

A coin weighs about one-third of an ounce, so fifty coins weigh a pound.

## Selling Equipment

Equipment fetches half its cost when sold. In contrast, trade goods and valuables such as gems and art objects retain their full market value.

## Weapons

Each weapon entry records:

- category: `Simple` or `Martial`
- whether it is `Melee` or `Ranged`
- damage and damage type
- properties
- mastery property
- weight
- cost

Weapon proficiency lets a creature add Proficiency Bonus to attack rolls with that weapon. Anyone can wield a weapon, but proficiency is what improves accuracy.

## Armor

Armor entries record:

- category: `Light`, `Medium`, `Heavy`, or `Shield`
- Armor Class calculation or bonus
- Strength threshold if applicable
- Stealth penalty if applicable
- don and doff times
- weight
- cost

## Tools

Tool descriptions define:

- the ability used with the tool
- what can be done with the `Utilize` action
- what can be crafted with the tool
- any variants that require separate proficiency

## Magic-Item Handling

The general `Equipment` chapter also establishes:

- identifying a magic item through `Identify`, a Short Rest, or experimentation
- attuning during a Short Rest
- no more than three attuned items at a time
- normal restrictions on wearing multiple items of the same kind
