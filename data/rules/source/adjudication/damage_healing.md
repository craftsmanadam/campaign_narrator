# Damage and Healing

Hit Points represent durability and the will to live.

## Damage Rolls

- Weapons, spells, and monster abilities define damage dice.
- Weapon attacks add the same ability modifier used for the attack roll to damage.
- Fixed damage that does not use a roll usually does not add an ability modifier unless a rule says otherwise.

## Critical Hits

On a critical hit:

- roll the attack's damage dice twice,
- add them together,
- then add normal modifiers.

Additional damage dice from other features are also doubled.

## Saving Throw Damage

- When an effect damages multiple targets with one save-based effect, roll damage once for all targets.
- "Half damage on a success" means half of the failed-save damage, rounded down.

## Damage Types

Damage types have meaning mainly because other rules care about them.

## Resistance, Vulnerability, Immunity

- Resistance halves damage of that type.
- Vulnerability doubles damage of that type.
- Immunity prevents that damage type or condition entirely.

Order of application:

1. bonuses, penalties, and multipliers,
2. Resistance,
3. Vulnerability.

Multiple instances of Resistance or Vulnerability to the same type do not stack.

## Healing

- Magic, rests, and certain items restore Hit Points.
- Healing cannot raise current Hit Points above the target's maximum.
- A melee attack that would reduce a creature to 0 can instead leave it at 1 HP and unconscious if the attacker chooses to knock it out.

## Temporary Hit Points

- Temporary Hit Points are lost before normal Hit Points.
- They do not stack.
- They usually last until depleted or until a Long Rest.
