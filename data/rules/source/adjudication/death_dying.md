# Death and Dying

When a creature drops to 0 Hit Points, it either dies immediately or falls unconscious.

## Instant Death Cases

- Many monsters die immediately at 0 HP.
- A creature dies if its Hit Point maximum becomes 0.
- A character dies from massive damage if leftover damage after reaching 0 equals or exceeds the Hit Point maximum.

## Falling Unconscious

If a character reaches 0 Hit Points and does not die instantly:

- it gains the Unconscious condition,
- it begins making Death Saving Throws at the start of its turns.

## Death Saving Throws

- Roll `1d20`.
- `10+` is a success.
- `9 or less` is a failure.
- Three successes: the character becomes Stable.
- Three failures: the character dies.
- A natural 1 counts as two failures.
- A natural 20 restores 1 Hit Point.

## Taking Damage at 0 HP

- Any damage at 0 HP causes one failed death save.
- Critical-hit damage at 0 HP causes two failures.
- Damage equal to or greater than the Hit Point maximum kills the character outright.

## Stabilizing

- A creature can use Help and succeed on a `DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine)` check to stabilize a creature at 0 HP.
- A stable creature stops making death saves, remains unconscious, and regains 1 HP after `1d4` hours if not healed sooner.
