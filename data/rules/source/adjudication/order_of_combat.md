# The Order of Combat

Combat is organized into rounds and turns.

- a round represents about 6 seconds
- during a round, each participant takes one turn in initiative order
- combat continues round by round until one side is defeated, flees, surrenders, or both sides agree to stop

Combat setup:

1. Establish positions.
2. Roll Initiative.
3. Take turns in Initiative order.
4. Repeat rounds until combat ends.

Initiative:

- each participant makes a Dexterity check to determine Initiative
- the GM can roll once for a group of identical creatures
- if a combatant is surprised when combat begins, that combatant has Disadvantage on the Initiative roll
- ties are broken by player choice among tied characters, GM choice among tied monsters, and GM choice when the tie crosses those categories

On a turn:

- a creature can move up to its Speed
- a creature can take one action
- the creature chooses whether to move first or act first
- the creature can communicate briefly without using movement or an action
- extended speech or persuasion attempts usually require an action
- one object interaction is free; more require Utilize

Combat can also be played on a square grid:

- each square is 5 feet
- movement is counted in 5-foot segments
- difficult terrain costs extra squares
- diagonal movement cannot cut through blocked corners
- range is counted by squares along the shortest route
