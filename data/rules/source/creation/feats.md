# Feats

Feats are organized in the SRD by category.

## Categories

- Origin Feats
- General Feats
- Fighting Style Feats
- Epic Boon Feats

The first-cut prototype includes the first three categories for normal play through level 5. Epic Boons remain excluded because they target level 19+ play.

## Feat Anatomy

Each feat can define:

- category,
- prerequisite,
- benefits,
- repeatability.

If a feat has a class prerequisite, the character must have at least one level in that class unless another rule says otherwise.

## Origin Feats in the SRD Slice

- Alert
  - adds Proficiency Bonus to Initiative
  - allows initiative swapping with a willing ally
- Magic Initiate
  - grants two cantrips and one level 1 spell from Cleric, Druid, or Wizard
  - the spell can be cast once per Long Rest without a slot
  - repeatable with a different spell list
- Savage Attacker
  - once per turn, reroll weapon damage dice and use either result
- Skilled
  - gain proficiency in any combination of three skills or tools
  - repeatable

## General and Fighting Style Examples Relevant by Level 5

- Ability Score Improvement at level 4+
- Grappler at level 4+ with Strength or Dexterity 13+
- Archery
- Defense
- Great Weapon Fighting
- Two-Weapon Fighting

## Implementation Notes

- Backgrounds grant the initial Origin feat.
- Level-based feat acquisition later in play should check prerequisites and repeatability.
- Level 4 feat choice matters for the level 1 to 5 prototype and should be persisted explicitly.
