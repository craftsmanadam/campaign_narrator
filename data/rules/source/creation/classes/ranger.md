# Ranger

## Core Traits

- Primary ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
- Hit Point Die: `d10`
- Saves: Strength, Dexterity
- Skills: choose `3` from Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, or Survival
- Armor: Light, Medium, Shields
- Weapons: Simple, Martial
- Starting equipment: choose two-weapon package, heavy-ranged package, or `155 GP`

## Level 1 to 5

- Level 1: `Spellcasting`, `Favored Enemy`, `Weapon Mastery`
- Level 2: `Deft Explorer`, `Fighting Style`
- Level 3: Ranger subclass
- Level 4: feat or Ability Score Improvement
- Level 5: `Extra Attack`

## Spellcasting Shape

- Ranger spellcasting starts at level 1 in this SRD
- Prepared level 1+ spells at level 1: 2
- At level 5, prepared spells: `6`
- Spellcasting ability: Wisdom
- Uses a Druidic Focus for many recommended builds
- Spell slots progress as `2` level-1 slots at level 1, `3` level-1 at level 2, `4/2` at level 5.

## Key Mechanics

- `Favored Enemy` centers the class around `Hunter's Mark`
- `Deft Explorer` supports travel and exploration identity
- `Weapon Mastery` helps the Ranger stay relevant as a martial combatant
- `Favored Enemy` always prepares `Hunter's Mark`; at level 1 it can be cast without a slot twice per Long Rest.
- `Fighting Style` arrives at level 2.

## Subclass in the SRD

`Hunter`

- Level 3: `Hunter's Lore`, `Hunter's Prey`
- Level 7: `Defensive Tactics`
- Level 11: `Superior Hunter's Prey`
- Level 15: `Superior Hunter's Defense`

## First-Cut Use

The Ranger is valuable for the prototype because it bridges:

- wilderness play,
- martial combat,
- and low-level spell support.

Make `Hunter's Mark` state and active quarry targeting explicit in runtime logic.
