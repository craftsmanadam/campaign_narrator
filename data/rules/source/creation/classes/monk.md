# Monk

## Core Traits

- Primary ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
- Hit Point Die: `d8`
- Saves: Strength, Dexterity
- Armor: none
- Weapons: Simple weapons and Martial weapons with the Light property

## Level 1 to 5

- Level 1: `Martial Arts`, `Unarmored Defense`
- Level 2: `Monk's Focus`, `Unarmored Movement`, `Uncanny Metabolism`
- Level 3: `Deflect Attacks`, Monk subclass
- Level 4: feat or Ability Score Improvement, `Slow Fall`
- Level 5: `Extra Attack`, `Stunning Strike`

## Core Combat Identity

- Bonus Action Unarmed Strike while meeting Monk weapon and armor restrictions
- Martial Arts die starts at `1d6`
- Can use Dexterity for unarmed and Monk-weapon attacks
- Unarmored Defense sets base AC to `10 + Dexterity modifier + Wisdom modifier`

## Focus Points

The Monk's main class resource powers:

- `Flurry of Blows`
- `Patient Defense`
- `Step of the Wind`

This should be tracked explicitly in runtime state.

## Subclass in the SRD

`Warrior of the Open Hand`

- Level 3: `Open Hand Technique`
- Level 6: `Wholeness of Body`
- Level 11: `Fleet Step`
- Level 17: `Quivering Palm`

## First-Cut Use

For the prototype, the Monk is important because it stresses:

- Bonus Action economy,
- unarmed combat rules,
- and condition-heavy tactical effects like Stunned and Prone.
