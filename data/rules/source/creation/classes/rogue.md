# Rogue

## Core Traits

- Primary ability: Dexterity
- Hit Point Die: `d8`
- Saves: Dexterity, Intelligence
- Armor: Light
- Weapons: Simple and eligible Martial weapons with Finesse or Light
- Tool: Thieves' Tools

## Level 1 to 5

- Level 1: `Expertise`, `Sneak Attack`, `Thieves' Cant`, `Weapon Mastery`
- Level 2: `Cunning Action`
- Level 3: Rogue subclass, `Steady Aim`
- Level 4: feat or Ability Score Improvement
- Level 5: `Cunning Strike`, `Uncanny Dodge`

## Sneak Attack

- Starts at `1d6`
- Applies once per turn with a Finesse or Ranged weapon hit when the Rogue has Advantage
- Also applies when an ally is adjacent to the target and the Rogue does not have Disadvantage

## Tactical Features

- `Cunning Action` gives Bonus Action Dash, Disengage, or Hide
- `Steady Aim` trades movement for Advantage on the next attack roll
- `Cunning Strike` turns Sneak Attack dice into control effects such as `Poison`, `Trip`, or `Withdraw`

## Subclass in the SRD

`Thief`

- Level 3: `Fast Hands`, `Second-Story Work`
- Level 9: `Supreme Sneak`
- Level 13: `Use Magic Device`
- Level 17: `Thief's Reflexes`

## First-Cut Use

The Rogue is one of the key classes for your evaluation scenarios because it stresses:

- stealth adjudication,
- lock and trap interactions,
- and machine-checkable state changes from social or tactical play.
