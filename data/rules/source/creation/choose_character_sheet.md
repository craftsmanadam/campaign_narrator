# Choose a Character Sheet

The SRD treats a character sheet as whatever record a player uses to track a character's details. It can be a printed sheet, a digital sheet, or plain paper. The important rule is not the format; it is that the player has a place to record class, origin, abilities, equipment, features, spells, and later changes from level advancement.

For this corpus, a character sheet should be able to capture:

- class, level, and Experience Points,
- species, background, alignment, and languages,
- ability scores and ability modifiers,
- Proficiency Bonus and proficiencies,
- hit points, Hit Dice, Armor Class, Speed, and size,
- feats, class features, subclass, and spellcasting details,
- equipment, coins, and other notable details.

The SRD assumes that the character sheet is updated as character creation proceeds and again whenever the character gains a level.
